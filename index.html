My Story

After graduating with a Bachelor of science degree with major in Computer Science and minor in Mathematics in 2012, I lacked an area of specialization and and in my quest to specialize in one area in IT, I tried to become a DBA and did a certificate program back home with the aim of getting certified in Oracle 11g but ended up losing interest in database administration, then I returned to Canada in 2019 and made plans to return to school and get back on track, so I enrolled in the Sheridan College Software development and network engineering program which went over computer programming and introduced me to computer networking along with network security. After 2 full time semesters and 2 part time semesters I dropped out with the aim of becoming a network administrator or breaking into the cybersecurity industry, this is because I developed a keen interest in computer networks and performing hacking exploits. This was totally new to me to a certain extent and grabbed my full attention.

I started off by acquiring Comptia A+ and Security+ certifications in 2020 but later realized my knowledge of computer networking was not strong enough, so I am currently studying for CCNA, I did consider the Comptia Network+ but after doing some internet research I found out CCNA has more value in the IT job market. My journey to becoming A+ certified involved watching Miker Meyers A+ videos on lynda.com, reading Mike Meyers A+ textbook and doing a review using Professor Messer's free youtube training videos. If you live in Mississauga, Toronto and other cities in the GTA, you can get free access to lynda.com using your library card. I aslo used Jason Dion's A+ practise exam course on Udemy. My computer science background helped a lot and to be honest the only challenging part of the A+ exams was the 220-1001 which had greater emphasis on computer hardware configuration, repair and troubleshooting.

My journey to becoming Security+ certified was a lot easier because I took Intro to Data Communication, Network Design and Network Security classes at Sheridan College, the Network Security class was based on Comptia Security+ objectives and this class used a Comptia Security+ study guide textbook by Mark Ciampa. We did not cover every single Security+ objective at Sheridan College, so in order to study I started off by watching Mike Chapple's lynda.com course which was very detailed but boring, I took lots of notes and since I enjoyed Mike Meyers A+ content, he is funny, highly brilliant and sparks your interest in IT, I purchased his udemy Security+ course and watched it. To top of off, I purchased and used Jason's Security+ practise test on udemy to guage my readiness and find out which areas I needed to review. I aced the Sheridan College Network Security class but the Security+ was no joke....anyway, I passed it on my first attempt.

As you already know, I'm studying for CCNA, I'll be using Neil Anderson's Udemy course, reading Wendell Odom's textbook Vol 1 & 2, then finally Boson software practise exams which is supposed to be harder than the actual CCNA exam from what I heard. I have free Access to David Bombal's Udemy course which is very long and will serve as a video review at speed 1.5x.
